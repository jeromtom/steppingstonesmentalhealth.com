Understanding the Problem

Only 42% of children classified by schools as Emotionally Disturbed graduate.
These symptoms of emotional problems are behaviors, which can look willful. Many ask: Is it can’t or is it won’t?
Positive Behavior Support Plans often look like discipline plans. We need to incorporate tools into these plans that support the child in achieving the desired behavior.
A child with an emotional disturbance is likely to miss many school days.
The illness robs valuable energy before the day even starts. [more]
